Domestic dogs

Domestic dogs are dogs that have been tamed and trained by humans. All domestic dogs are related to wolves. Humans began to tame wolves and make them their companions about 12,000 years ago. Over the years, dogs were kept as hunting, herding, and guard dogs. They were also used in sport and for companionship, as pets. Today there are about 450 different breeds of domestic dogs that come in many shapes, sizes, hair lengths, and colors. While many make lovable and loyal pets, others are trained to do particular jobs. Some, such as the Labrador retriever are used as guide dogs for the blind. Others, such as the St. Bernard are trained for rescue work.

Gundogs were developed to carry out specific tasks and to work closely with hunters. The Labrador Retriever, shown here, was trained to collect fallen prey and bring it back to the hunter. Today, Labradors make excellent pets and are used as guide dogs for the blind.

The terrier group gets its name from the Latin word terra, meaning “earth”. This is because these dogs were originally used to hunt pests that live underground, for example, rats. Today, terriers make popular pets. This is an English Toy Terrier.

Working dogs are bred for strength and stamina, and many of them can live outdoors in all weathers. They are used to herd other animals, such as sheep, and as guard dogs, and can make excellent pets. The Border Collie shown here is a very energetic and super-intelligent breed of dog

One of the largest breeds of domestic dog, the St Bernard was bred by monks at the St Bernard hospice in the Swiss Alps. It is well known as a mountain rescue dog and has a calm and gentle personality.

Primitive dogs are a varied group of dogs that share wolf-like characteristics and are not completely domesticated. The Portuguese Podengo is a primitive dog that has traditionally been used for hunting. It is the national dog of Portugal.

Scent hounds are dogs that have an excellent sense of smell. They track prey by following scent. These dogs have a natural ability to pick up a trail, even if it is a few days old. The Poitevin originally comes from western France.

The Dalmatian is is the only breed of spotted dog. It usually has black spots on white fur, but Dalmatian puppies don't have any spots at all! It is an energetic dog and makes an excellent pet. They were originally used as “carriage dogs”, running along or under horse-drawn carriages in the 19th century.

The smallest dog breed in the world is the Chihuahua. It originated in Mexico and is named after the Mexican state of Chihuahua. The Chihuahua is one of the most popular breeds in the USA.

Dogs made from two different types of dog are known as crossbreeds. The Labradoodle shown here is a popular crossbreed. It is a mix between a Labrador Retriever and a Poodle.

The Alaskan Malamute has long, thick fur, a wolf-like appearance and is suited to living in very cold places. The Alaskan Malamute was once used to pull heavy loads and travel great distances across the snow. Today it is often used in dogsled races.

Sight hounds, such as this Polish Greyhound, are dogs hunt using their keen eyesight. Like other dogs in this group, the Polish Greyhound is a speedy runner.

Also known as mongrels or mutts, mixed-breed dogs are widespread and have parents that are not pure breeds. No two mixed-breed dogs are the same.
